Is getting the right stationery for your big day stressing you out?

What makes something beautiful? Why do we look at a Mercedes or an Audi and instantly know that they are expensive cars driven by wealthy people? What makes a Waitrose shopping bag look more sophisticated than one from Lidl?

We all know beauty when we see it, but if we have to explain what makes something beautiful, elegant or sophisticated... well it's kind of hard to put your finger on it:

...you just know.

If I ask you to compare several dresses and pick out the most expensive designer dress, it probably wouldn't take long for you to choose correctly.

And if I ask you the differences between them you'll be able to pick out certain things: maybe the quality of the fabric...

...or the diamond-encrusted buttons...

But while some of the ingredients of beauty are blazingly obvious, it's usually the small, subtle nuances---the things you can't put your finger on--that really make the difference.

The gentle, soft click of a Mercedes' door closing...

The lighting and shadows of that award-winning photograph...

The way the dress falls on a supermodel's body in the evening breeze:

Exquisite beauty is all about attention to detail--all the little dots that, when placed on the paper in the right places, join together to give you a beautiful picture.

And that's why, if you want wedding stationery that really stands out from the crowd, you deserve to know the subtle secrets that will set your stationery head and shoulders above the rest...

Has your stationery gone to Finishing School?

There's nothing more upsetting than attractive stationery spoiled by poor finishing: edges that aren't cut straight or one side that you're sure looks slightly longer than the other...

it'll eat away at you until you get that ruler out and check for yourself!

Unfortunately smaller suppliers often use manual guillotines to cut and score your stationery which make accurate cutting difficult.

Because, like you, I am a perfectionist, I use an industrial guillotine with laser-guided cutting and mechanical scoring for razor-sharp edges and clean folds.
